The Big Beautiful Bill Act

💰 Taxes — What Changed
Permanent extensions (from 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act):
Individual tax rates from 2017 are now permanent — they were set to expire end of 2025 LegiScan

New deductions (temporary, expire 2028 unless noted):
No tax on tips — up to $25,000/year for workers earning under $150,000 LegiScan
No tax on overtime — deduction up to $12,500 ($25,000 if married) for overtime pay, phases out above $150,000 income LegiScan
Auto loan interest deduction — up to $10,000/year for new US-assembled vehicles purchased 2025–2028, phases out above $100,000 income LegiScan
SALT deduction raised from $10,000 to $40,000 for taxpayers earning under $500,000, reverting to $10,000 after five years LegiScan
Senior deduction — up to $6,000 for seniors, phasing out above $75,000 income, expires 2028 LegiScan
Charitable deduction for non-itemizers — up to $1,000 ($2,000 married), effective January 2026 LegiScan

Child & Family:
Child tax credit increased from $2,000 to $2,200 per child, indexed to inflation, but only for US citizens or qualifying noncitizens LegiScan
Trump accounts created — tax-deferred savings accounts parents can open for children, expires 2028 LegiScan
Adoption tax credit — up to $5,000 now refundable starting January 2025 LegiScan

Other tax changes:
1% tax on remittances sent abroad LegiScan
EV tax credits repealed; most other clean energy credits restricted or phased out faster BEA
Health insurance premium tax credits cut by about 20% through tighter eligibility rules BEA

🏥 Healthcare — Major Cuts
Medicaid cut by approximately 12%, with the CBO estimating 10.9 million Americans will lose health insurance coverage LegiScan
The lowest 10% of earners are projected to see incomes fall by 3.1% by 2034, primarily due to cuts to Medicaid and food aid LegiScan

🍎 Food Assistance (SNAP)
Work requirements for SNAP benefits expanded, and states are now responsible for some costs of the food assistance program LegiScan

🌱 Clean Energy
The law raises about $500 billion over a decade by reducing green energy credits by roughly half — EV credits and residential energy product credits are repealed, while others are restricted or phased out sooner BEA

🏗️ Defense & Border
$150 billion in new defense spending and $150 billion for border enforcement and deportations LegiScan
ICE funding increased from $10 billion to over $100 billion by 2029, making it the single most funded federal law enforcement agency LegiScan

📊 Big Picture
The CBO estimates the law will increase the budget deficit by $2.8 trillion by 2034 LegiScan
The highest 10% of earners are projected to see incomes rise by 2.7% by 2034 due to tax cuts, while the lowest 10% see incomes fall 3.1% due to program cuts
